    About

    San Diego is a bustling port city in southern California that has an amazing view of San Diego Bay and the Pacific Ocean. It is the second-largest city in the state and the seat of San Diego County, home to more than 1.3 million people. Wide valleys, canyons, and mesas are among the topographical features that define San Diego's terrain. The terrain gets hillier in the north. There are two distinct sections of the city: a larger area in the north and east and a smaller area in the southeast that stretches towards the Mexican border. Its year-round mild, sunny weather makes it a great place for outdoor enthusiasts, with a plethora of recreational opportunities available both inland and along its picturesque coastline.

    The rich history of San Diego begins in the 16th century, when native Diegueño, Luiseño, Cahuilla, and Cupeño peoples lived there. Following European colonization and exploration, the region was named San Miguel in 1542 by Spanish explorer Juan Rodríguez Cabrillo. The area was first settled by Europeans in 1602, when it was renamed San Diego de Alcalá de Henares. A Spanish presidio and mission were established there. After the Santa Fe Railway arrived in 1885 and the defense industries grew during and after World War II, San Diego prospered as a center of trade, industry, and military activity over time. San Diego's economy is diverse today, with industries including manufacturing, biotechnology, tourism, defense, and telecommunications included. Its vibrant arts scene, varied population, and abundance of educational institutions all contribute to its cultural vibrancy, which makes it an exciting and sought-after destination for travel, employment, and residence.

    San Diego Stats

    • Population: Over 1.3 million residents.
    • Year San Diego was incorporated: 1850.
    • Region: Southern California, situated along the Pacific Ocean at San Diego Bay.
    • Classification: Primarily urban, with some suburban areas.
    • Average income level: Generally comparable to or slightly higher than the rest of the state, reflecting its diverse economy and industries.
