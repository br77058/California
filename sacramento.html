    About

    California's capital city of Sacramento is located in the north-central region of the state, tucked away in the Sacramento Valley. It is one of the oldest incorporated communities in California, having been incorporated in 1850. Situated at the confluence of the American and Sacramento rivers, the city is roughly 45 miles north of Stockton and 90 miles northeast of San Francisco. Amidst the region's long, hot summers and cool, damp winters, Sacramento's flat terrain gradually rises to the east towards the foothills of the Sierra Nevada, providing a picturesque setting.

    Sacramento, home to more than 500,000 people, is the center of a thriving metropolitan region that spans four counties. Citrus Heights, Folsom, Carmichael, and Roseville are a few of the suburbs that contribute to the vibrant cultural and economic environment of the city. Sacramento has a rich history as a major trading post and an agricultural hub during the Gold Rush. After becoming the capital of the state in 1854, it experienced explosive growth spurred by the mining industry and the expansion of the transportation network. Sacramento's economy has expanded over the years to include a variety of sectors, including tourism, government services, agriculture, and electronics manufacturing. Famous sites like the restored Old Sacramento historic district and the Roman golden-domed State Capitol serve as reminders of the city's rich history.
